BioTrack is a collaborative project to assess and monitor biodiversity hotspots where marine species share habitat. By integrating satellite and acoustic tracking of marine animals with remotely sensed environmental data and modeling techniques, BioTrack aims to map these hotspots and their intersections with areas of human use such as marine sanctuaries and wind energy leasing blocks.

{{< youtube 39pzn1paZEA >}}

Knowledge about marine species hotspots and how they are changing will provide opportunities for prioritizing spatial protections, planning for multiple use of ocean spaces, and monitoring ecosystem responses to environmental change. Data are made available through an interactive portal jointly hosted by MBON and the Animal Tracking Network (ATN). The current focus is the Western North Atlantic, Gulf of Mexico, and Caribbean Sea, with a plan to scale to other regions.

## Frequently asked questions

**What are the specific project objectives?**

1. Identify and predict the location and timing of biodiversity hotspots of marine species.
2. Characterize the environmental conditions underlying these hotspots.
3. Map hotspots with management areas and maritime jurisdictions to develop conservation options and increase fishing efficiency by reducing bycatch.
4. Monitor and model how these hotspots change over time in response to environmental change.

**What are the end products and deliverables?**

1. Multi-author scientific papers — data contributors will be co-authors.
2. Data summary visualizations and infographics for management bodies.
3. Stronger linkages between MBON and the ATN.

**What are the benefits of collaborating?**

1. Assistance with tracking data management and archiving.
2. Broader impact outreach through public-facing visualizations.
3. Co-authorship in publications.
4. Application of data as a management tool.
5. Building collaborative networks and supporting graduate student research.

**What data are you looking for?**

Acoustic and satellite tagging data from past or current projects — any marine species that have been tagged (marine mammals, seabirds, teleosts, sharks, reptiles). Information needed includes species, deployment location, and dates.

**How is my data protected?**

Raw data are protected to the extent desired by the contributor. See the [BioTrack data management plan](docs/biotrack-data-management-plan.pdf) for details.

**Will my data become public?**

Data are aggregated and synthesized into products. Individual contributors are free to use their data in any other research or publications independently — participation in BioTrack does not preclude stand-alone manuscripts.
