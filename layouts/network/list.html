Get involved

Join the MBON network

MBON is an open community of practice — scientists, resource managers, data providers, and end-users all contribute and benefit. Whether you run a long-term observing program, develop data products, or manage marine resources, there is a role for you in the network.

Contribute observations

Publish biodiversity data to OBIS following MBON standard protocols. Regional nodes welcome new data providers who can commit to FAIR data sharing.

Join a working group

Active working groups cover eDNA, remote sensing, BioTrack, BioSound, indicators, and data management. Meetings are open; contact the working group lead to join.

Partner at the global level

MBON is a thematic node of GEO BON and leads the Marine Life 2030 programme of the UN Ocean Decade. International organizations can partner through the MBON Secretariat (AIR Centre, Azores).
